'Cancer Sutra' shows the sexy side of checking your private bits

447
Shares
447
Share on Facebook Share Tweet on Twitter Share Share
What's This?
By Matt Petronzio5 hours ago

Editor's note: The below images may be considered NSFW.

Who says cancer prevention can't be sexy?

The Cancer Sutra, a new website and ebook from the Bull-White House and Stupid Cancer, is tackling the taboos surrounding the cancer conversation.

See also: Wedding, Career, Chemo: When Cancer Derails the Millennial Dream

Showcasing colorful, racy pics of 32 sexual positions inspired by the sacred Hindu text Kama Sutra, the project cheekily raises awareness by showing how partners can check each other for early signs of skin, breast, testicular and prostate cancer.

"

This project exists to help save lives. One tweak, thrust, moan and pant at a time
This project exists to help save lives. One tweak, thrust, moan and pant at a time," the splash page proudly states. Ultimately, it's a simple reminder that everyone needs to take control of his or her health, specifically checking themselves and getting screened regularly.

The idea came from the indisputable fact that the earlier you detect cancer, the best chance there is of treating it successfully. But self-consciousness and lack of knowledge of our own bodies often get in the way. So the team behind The Cancer Sutra thought we might as well check each other.

"We want to show that you can put across a serious message in a way that will bring joy," Matthew Bull, founder of the Bull-White House, tells Mashable. "I hope people will consider it a brave initiative for brave people. Simple as that."
